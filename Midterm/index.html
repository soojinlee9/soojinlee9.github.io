Why Whales?

Whales amaze us with their vast size and complex ways of communication as they roam the oceans. Despite living in water, they breathe air just like us and they are warm-blooded mammals who nurse their young. Whales play an important role in the wellbeing of the marine ecosystem. And although they are at the top of their food chain, about half of the great whale species are endangered. Factors such as commercial whaling, ship activity and climate change are the biggest threats to whales.

The North Atlantic and North Pacific Right whales are ranked 3 and 4 on the most endangered whale species list. There are currently fewer than 300 North Atlantic Right whales swimming in our oceans.
